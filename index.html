Christopher D. Hare

Associate Professor of Political Science at the University of California, Davis.

Research

My research focuses on ideology and voting behavior in the mass public, campaign strategy, politics and religion, and political polarization. Methodologically, I apply measurement theory, ideal point estimation, Bayesian methods, and machine learning to model political behavior.

Classes

  • Scientific Study of Politics
  • Political Polarization
  • Applied Bayesian Modeling

About

I am from the Mississippi Gulf Coast (Long Beach, Mississippi) and have been a lifelong Saints and Southern Miss Golden Eagles fan. I received my PhD in political science from the University of Georgia and my BA and MA in political science from the University of Southern Mississippi. I started as an assistant professor of political science at the University of California, Davis, in July 2015.
